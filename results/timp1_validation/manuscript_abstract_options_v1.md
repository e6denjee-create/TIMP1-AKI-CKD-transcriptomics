# Manuscript Abstract Options v1

## Option 1: Structured, data-forward

### Background

Maladaptive kidney repair involves persistent tubular injury, inflammation,
senescence, and extracellular matrix remodeling. We evaluated the
cross-cohort transcriptomic context of TIMP1 in human kidney disease.

### Methods

Three discovery bulk cohorts (GSE139061, GSE30718, and GSE66494) and an
independent microdissected tubular cohort (GSE180394) were analyzed separately.
We tested TIMP1 disease-control expression, nine prespecified signatures,
disease-only genome-wide correlations, and a conserved module. GSE180394
validation included diagnosis-removal, alternative-control, and
diagnosis-adjusted analyses.

### Results

TIMP1 was directionally elevated in all discovery cohorts. In GSE180394,
TIMP1 was higher in 44 disease samples than in nine living-donor controls
(difference = 1.318; Hedges' g = 1.569; P = 8.01 x 10^-5). All nine
signatures were positively and FDR-significantly correlated with TIMP1 in
all-sample and disease-only analyses. A 421-gene stringent disease-only module
was identified in discovery datasets; its external score correlated with
TIMP1 in all samples (rho = 0.879) and disease samples (rho = 0.850). All 12
core genes were positively correlated with TIMP1 externally. All 22 evaluated
features remained positive in leave-one-diagnosis-out analyses, and 21 of 22
remained FDR-significant after diagnosis adjustment.

### Conclusions

TIMP1 is associated with tubular injury-repair, ECM remodeling, inflammatory
activation, senescence, fibrosis-related programs, and a conserved
disease-only transcriptomic module across human kidney disease cohorts. These
results support TIMP1 as a candidate component of a broader maladaptive
injury-response state but do not establish prediction, diagnostic utility,
tissue specificity, or causality.

## Option 2: Biological narrative

Incomplete repair after kidney injury is characterized by persistent tubular
stress and coordinated inflammatory and matrix-remodeling responses. To define
the transcriptomic context of TIMP1 within this process, we integrated three
human kidney discovery cohorts with an independent microdissected tubular
cohort. TIMP1 was directionally elevated across GSE139061, GSE30718, and
GSE66494. In GSE180394, comprising 44 kidney-disease and nine healthy
living-donor tubular samples, TIMP1 was higher in disease (Hedges'
g = 1.569; P = 8.01 x 10^-5). TIMP1 correlated with nine prespecified
programs covering tubular injury, maladaptive repair, ECM remodeling,
collagen, TGF-beta signaling, inflammation, immune activation, senescence,
and fibrosis. Most associations persisted when analyses were restricted to
disease samples. A conserved 421-gene disease-only module was strongly
correlated with TIMP1 in GSE180394 (rho = 0.850), and all 12 core genes
retained positive external correlations. The pattern remained positive after
sequential removal of major diagnosis categories; 21 of 22 signature, module,
and core-gene associations remained FDR-significant after diagnosis
adjustment. These findings place TIMP1 within a reproducible tubular
injury-repair and cytoskeletal-inflammatory-matrix program. The results are
association-based and require multi-donor cellular, spatial, and experimental
validation.

## Option 3: Conservative reviewer-facing

Public kidney transcriptomic datasets vary in platform, disease etiology, and
tissue composition, limiting conclusions from single-cohort differential
expression. We used a platform-specific cross-dataset design to assess TIMP1
expression and associated biological programs without pooling absolute
expression values. Three discovery cohorts contained 120 disease and 28
control samples in total, and GSE180394 provided an external validation set
of 44 heterogeneous kidney-disease and nine living-donor microdissected
tubular samples. TIMP1 was directionally higher in all discovery cohorts and
was elevated in GSE180394 (difference = 1.318; Hedges' g = 1.569;
P = 8.01 x 10^-5). Nine prespecified signature scores excluded TIMP1 from
their gene membership. All external disease-only correlations were positive
and FDR-significant. A stringent module defined by positive FDR-supported
TIMP1 correlations in at least two discovery datasets contained 421 genes;
its GSE180394 score correlated with TIMP1 at rho = 0.850 among disease
samples. All 12 genes supported in all three discovery cohorts retained
positive external correlations. Diagnosis-removal analyses preserved the
direction of all 22 evaluated features, and diagnosis adjustment retained FDR
support for 21. TIMP1 is therefore reproducibly associated with tubular
injury-repair, inflammatory, senescence, ECM-remodeling, and fibrosis-related
programs. Cross-sectional design and incomplete clinical covariates preclude
claims regarding longitudinal transition, clinical performance, or causal
activity.
