# TIMP1 Validation Report v2

## 1. Executive summary

The current evidence supports TIMP1 as a candidate associated with kidney
injury-repair, extracellular matrix (ECM) remodeling, inflammation, cellular
senescence, and maladaptive repair programs relevant to AKI-to-CKD transition.
The main evidence comes from three independently analyzed bulk cohorts:
GSE139061, GSE30718, and GSE66494.

TIMP1 was directionally elevated in disease in all three cohorts, with
BH-adjusted significance in GSE30718 and GSE66494. Across nine prespecified
signatures, all 27 all-sample correlations were positive and FDR-significant;
25 of 27 disease-only correlations were FDR-significant. Disease-only
genome-wide analysis identified a relaxed module of 1,343 genes and a
stringent module of 421 genes supported by FDR < 0.05 in at least two cohorts.
Twelve genes had positive FDR support in all three cohorts.

Sensitivity analyses retained wound-response, inflammatory, adhesion,
cytoskeletal, senescence, and selected ECM-associated signals after removing
prespecified signature genes or ECM/fibrosis markers. These results are based
on a labeled curated offline enrichment subset. Full Hallmark, GO BP, GO CC,
Reactome, and KEGG collection retrieval was attempted but could not be
completed because the MSigDB host was unavailable from the local environment.

Single-cell evidence currently provides exploratory localization in one donor,
not patient-level replication. The project does not establish TIMP1 as a
kidney-specific marker, a clinically validated diagnostic biomarker, or a
causal driver.

## 2. Evidence hierarchy

### Main evidence

1. Within-cohort bulk TIMP1 expression differences.
2. Bulk TIMP1 correlations with nine prespecified biological signatures.
3. A cross-dataset, disease-only TIMP1-associated correlation module.
4. Sensitivity analyses using relaxed and stringent module definitions and
   gene-removal analyses.

### Supportive evidence

The available GSE210622 single-cell object supports exploratory localization
of TIMP1 expression and a TIMP1-high tubular-cell-associated program. It
contains only one donor, so cell-level tests cannot substitute for donor-level
replication.

### Not yet supported

- Kidney specificity has not been established.
- Diagnostic performance has not been clinically validated.
- Causal regulation of fibrosis or maladaptive repair has not been established.
- A TIMP1-centered correlation network is not a regulatory network.
- Single-donor cell-level P values do not establish clinical relevance.

## 3. Available bulk datasets

| Dataset | Context | Disease | Control | TIMP1 group difference | Hedges' g | BH-adjusted P |
|---|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| GSE139061 | AKI | 39 | 9 | 1.086 | 0.415 | 0.378 |
| GSE30718 | AKI | 28 | 11 | 1.062 | 1.293 | 0.000326 |
| GSE66494 | CKD/fibrosis | 53 | 8 | 0.417 | 0.449 | 0.00905 |

Absolute expression values were not pooled across platforms. No independent
fourth bulk cohort was found in the project. The candidate cohort types,
inclusion criteria, exclusions, and extraction fields are documented in
`candidate_external_bulk_datasets.md`.

## 4. Bulk signature evidence

The nine signatures were ECM remodeling, collagen formation, TGF-beta
signaling, inflammation, tubular injury, maladaptive repair, cellular
senescence, fibrosis, and immune activation. TIMP1 was excluded from signature
calculation to avoid mechanical self-correlation.

- All 27 all-sample TIMP1-signature correlations were positive and
  FDR-significant.
- In disease-only samples, 25 of 27 correlations were FDR-significant.
- GSE30718 and GSE66494 retained FDR support for all nine disease-only
  associations.
- In GSE139061, disease-only collagen formation and cellular senescence
  correlations remained positive but did not reach FDR < 0.05.

The persistence of disease-only associations indicates that the results are
not explained only by case-control separation. They remain observational and
may also reflect severity, cell composition, inflammation, or shared upstream
injury programs.

## 5. TIMP1-associated module

Genome-wide Spearman correlations were calculated separately in disease-only
samples. The relaxed module required positive direction and nominal P < 0.05
in at least two datasets. The stringent module required positive direction and
FDR < 0.05 in at least two datasets.

| Sensitivity definition | Genes |
|---|---:|
| Relaxed module | 1,343 |
| Stringent module | 421 |
| Relaxed after removing all signature genes | 1,291 |
| Stringent after removing all signature genes | 389 |
| Relaxed after removing ECM/fibrosis markers | 1,323 |
| Stringent after removing ECM/fibrosis markers | 414 |

The large retained modules show that the cross-dataset association is not
entirely generated by the prespecified signatures or a small group of
canonical ECM/fibrosis markers. This does not identify direction of regulation
or distinguish direct from indirect relationships.

## 6. Enrichment and sensitivity analysis

The preferred enrichment stack was evaluated:

- `msigdbr` 26.1.0 was installed.
- `clusterProfiler`, `ReactomePA`, and `org.Hs.eg.db` were not installed in the
  project library.
- Retrieval of Hallmark, GO BP, GO CC, Reactome, and KEGG collections through
  `msigdbr` failed because the environment could not resolve the MSigDB
  download host.

The failures are recorded in `missing_data_log.csv` and
`comprehensive_enrichment_status.csv`. No full database-wide enrichment result
is claimed.

An explicitly labeled curated offline subset was used for reproducible
sensitivity analysis. The relaxed and stringent modules were associated with
ECM organization, collagen formation, response to wounding, inflammatory and
NF-kappa B-related signaling, focal adhesion, membrane/cytoskeletal programs,
and cellular senescence. After removal of all prespecified signature genes,
the smaller residual enrichment emphasized response to wounding, focal
adhesion, membrane, and inflammatory programs. After removal of ECM/fibrosis
markers, inflammatory, senescence, adhesion, and wound-response associations
remained.

This pattern supports a broader injury-repair-associated program correlated
with TIMP1, while the reduced number of enriched terms after gene removal
shows that some pathway coherence depends on the prespecified biological gene
sets. Confirmation with complete public gene-set collections remains needed.

## 7. Three-cohort core module

Twelve genes had positive TIMP1 correlations with FDR < 0.05 in all three
datasets:

`TMSB10`, `IFITM3`, `RAB31`, `MGP`, `PEA15`, `TUBA1B`, `SERPING1`, `FBLIM1`,
`TAGLN2`, `TGM2`, `TUBA1A`, and `PFN1`.

Their median Spearman rho values ranged from approximately 0.56 to 0.77. The
genes span cytoskeletal organization, membrane trafficking, inflammatory
response, complement regulation, adhesion, wound repair, and matrix-associated
biology. `MGP` and `TGM2` provide direct contextual links to matrix biology,
while several other genes are more consistent with activated, stressed, or
repair-associated cell states. These annotations are contextual biological
summaries, not evidence that the genes are directly regulated by TIMP1.

Detailed values and annotations are available in
`core_module_genes_summary.csv`.

## 8. Single-cell validation status

GSE210622 contains a complete local expression object, sample metadata, and
computational cell-type annotations, but only one donor is represented.
GSE267242 has local barcode and feature files but lacks a matching expression
matrix, cell metadata, donor identifiers, disease/time-point annotations, and
cell-type annotations.

The one-donor analysis is supportive and exploratory. The TIMP1-high tubular
contrast is compatible with a maladaptive repair-associated state, but the
75th percentile of tubular TIMP1 expression was zero and the comparison is
strongly influenced by detected versus undetected expression. Donor-level
pseudobulk validation remains necessary.

## 9. Limitations

1. Only three bulk cohorts are available, and they are cross-sectional,
   heterogeneous, and platform-specific.
2. GSE139061 did not show a statistically significant bulk TIMP1 group
   difference.
3. The cohorts do not longitudinally follow the same patients from AKI to CKD.
4. Correlations may be affected by cell composition, disease severity,
   treatment, technical variation, and unmeasured covariates.
5. Signature gene sets overlap and are not statistically independent.
6. The current sensitivity enrichment uses a targeted offline subset rather
   than complete Hallmark/GO/Reactome/KEGG collections.
7. No independent external fourth cohort has yet replicated the module.
8. Single-cell evidence is limited to one donor and is vulnerable to
   pseudoreplication.
9. No perturbation or spatial experiment establishes direct TIMP1-mediated
   regulation.

## 10. Manuscript claim boundaries

Appropriate language:

- TIMP1 is reproducibly elevated in kidney injury/fibrosis datasets.
- TIMP1 is associated with ECM remodeling, maladaptive repair, senescence,
  inflammation, and TGF-beta-related programs.
- A disease-only cross-dataset module is correlated with TIMP1 and is
  associated with injury-repair, inflammatory, cytoskeletal, and
  matrix-remodeling programs.
- TIMP1-high tubular cells may represent an exploratory maladaptive
  repair-associated state.
- TIMP1 is a candidate for additional AKI-to-CKD mechanistic and patient-level
  validation.

The manuscript should state that kidney specificity, clinical diagnostic
performance, and causal activity are not established by the current data.
