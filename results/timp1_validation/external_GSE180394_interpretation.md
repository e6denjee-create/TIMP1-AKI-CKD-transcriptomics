# External bulk validation: GSE180394

GSE180394 was included as an independent external tubulointerstitial
kidney-disease cohort. The primary comparison used 44 heterogeneous
kidney-disease samples and 9 healthy living-donor controls. Six unaffected
tumor-nephrectomy samples were excluded from the primary contrast.

TIMP1 was elevated in disease samples (group difference 1.318, Hedges' g
1.569, P = 8.01e-05). All nine signature correlations were positive and
FDR-significant in both all-sample and disease-only analyses. The stringent
TIMP1-module score was strongly correlated with TIMP1 in all samples
(rho = 0.879) and disease-only samples (rho = 0.850).

All 12 core genes were positively correlated with TIMP1 in both scopes. All
12 were FDR-significant in all samples, and 10 were FDR-significant among
disease-only samples. Disease-only FBLIM1 and PFN1 correlations remained
positive but did not reach FDR < 0.05.

The results support TIMP1 as a candidate associated with tubular
injury-repair, ECM remodeling, inflammatory activation, senescence, and
fibrosis-related programs. The cohort is cross-sectional and etiologically
heterogeneous and does not establish longitudinal AKI-to-CKD transition,
clinical diagnostic performance, or direct regulation.
